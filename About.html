Rick Allan: From Skeptic to Speaker


Once an atheist attending church only to support my wife, I embarked on a journey to find evidence for my worldview. I expected to uncover weak arguments—but what I found surprised me. Experts on both sides examined the same evidence and yet reached opposing conclusions. As I wrestled with this, I ultimately encountered an undeniable force: truth.

My investigation began with the most fundamental question—does God exist? The evidence overwhelmingly pointed to a creator. Once I concluded that belief in a creator was reasonable, my focus shifted to the world’s major religions. After careful examination, I found the evidence for Christianity uniquely compelling, while other religious traditions struggled to match its historical and evidential foundation.

Today, I share my findings in a variety of settings, from small gatherings to conferences with over 500 attendees. My talks resonate with:

• Skeptics, who may find themselves surprised by the strength of the evidence.

• Christians, who gain confidence in the foundations of their faith.

I have a particular passion for speaking at churches and Christian events, where many, like I once was, attend out of routine or social obligation, unaware of the rich evidence supporting their faith. My mission is to reach these "church attendees" – those who may quietly question or remain indifferent to the beliefs they encounter each week. Many are unaware of the substantial case for Christianity. My goal is to present the evidence that transformed my understanding, helping them see that faith and reason are not mutually exclusive.

If you’re looking for a speaker who can engage both skeptics and believers with clear, visually rich, evidence-based talks, I’d love to be a part of your event.
