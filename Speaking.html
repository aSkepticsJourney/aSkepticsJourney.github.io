Speaking Topics

A Book Like No Other

(~30 mins)

This talk presents evidence that the Bible is a unique, authentic text that has remained materially unchanged over centuries. It stands as a work of non-fiction, reflecting real places, people, and explicit historical details.

Christianity - Fact or Fiction?

(~30-50 mins)

Explores the historical and textual credibility of Christianity by examining the enduring integrity of its scriptures, their grounding in verifiable history, the nature of firsthand narratives, and the strength of the resurrection claim against alternative explanations:
• Authenticity: The Bible's consistency over time.
• Non-Fiction: Real people, real places, explicit history.
• Eyewitness Accounts: The Gospels offer firsthand perspectives, with expected variations.
• Resurrection: Why all other resurrection theories fall short.

Science Says whaaat?

(~50 mins)

Considers whether recent scientific discoveries of the intricate structure of life and the precise conditions of the universe point to intentional design rather than random chance:
• Design in Life: DNA and molecular machines as evidence of intentional design.
• Design in the World: Big bang, cosmic order, and fine-tuning suggesting purposeful creation.

Cut From A Different Cloth

(~50 mins)

Examines what sets Christianity apart by exploring the unmatched character of its founder, the unexpected nature of its central message, the foresight of ancient predictions, compelling historical and cultural distinctives, and the extraordinary event at its core:
• Founder: Jesus is unlike any other religious figure.
• Salvation: A uniquely free, unearned gift.
• Prophecies: Predictions made centuries before fulfillment.
• Rises Above: Unique qualities like the early inclusion of women as leaders and the mystery of the Shroud of Turin.
• Resurrection: Miracles and the singularity of the resurrection claim.

Evidence for God 

(~50 mins)

Explores whether the complexity of life and the universe, along with human experiences of morality, consciousness, and suffering, point beyond the natural world to a purposeful, intelligent source:
• Design in Life: DNA, molecular machines, origin of life.
• Design in the World: Big bang, laws of nature, fine-tuning.
• Supernatural: Morality, afterlife, consciousness.
• Evil and Suffering: Understanding this common challenge to faith.

Evidence For Christianity

(~50 mins)

This presentation explores the transformative power of Christianity from its origins to today, the reliability of its sources, the credibility of the Gospel eyewitness accounts, and the unique nature of Christianity among world religions.:
• Radical Change: The transformative impact of early Christianity through today.
• Sources: The reliability of the Bible and other independent historical accounts.
• Eyewitness Testimony: The Gospels are eyewitness accounts which can be tested for truthfulness.
• Unique: Christianity stands out among all religions

Seminar - Evidence for God and Christianity

(2-4 hours)

A thorough, in-depth exploration of the evidence for God and Christianity that transformed my perspective from skepticism to faith, covering the same topics as the individual seminars, but with greater detail and insight.

All presentations are visually engaging, incorporating video clips and a touch of humor.

Content and length can be customized to fit your event needs.

Each presentation opens and closes with a personal testimonial of my journey.
